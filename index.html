ABOUT

Minye Yang completed the bachelor’s degree from Huazhong University of Science and Technology in Optical and Electronic Information, Hubei, China, in 2014, and received the M.Sc. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Wayne State University, Detroit, MI, in 2019. He is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Illinois at Chicago, Chicago, IL, USA. His research focuses on applied electromagnetics, RF, microwave antennas and circuits, biomedical sensing, metasurfaces, wireless sensors and integrated systems.

Reserach Experiences

Ultrasensitive Optical and RF Sensors in Pairy Time-Symmetry

  • The project aims to develop theoretical model for analysis of spectral singularities in PT-symmetry such as the coherent perfect absorber-laser (CPAL) point, exceptional point (EP), and divergent EP. We practically design and fabricate the corresponding devices and observe the CPAL, EP, and DEP phenomena. In addition, we apply such spectral singularities to the wireless biomedical sensing applications, such as intracranial pressure monitoring.

Physically Unclonable Functions in Optics and RF for Cyber Securities

  • The Physically Unclonable Functions (PUFs), serving as true random number generators, are of great imporance in the IoT era. The PUF based on the spectral singular points of PT-symmetry takes use of the extreme sensitive of the system toward unwanted perturbations induced during the fabrication process. In other word, tiny distrubances occurred to the lumped elements in PT-system will cause drastically, and randomly shifted operating statuses, forming a high quality PUF. In addition, we also employ the graphene devices to construt PUFs, as the graphene layers are highly sensitive to external environments, such as surface absorbates, unwanted doping during the fabrication. Those disturbances may result in unique electrical properties of graphene devices and thereby leading to perfect PUFs.
